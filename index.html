Traveling with your Glider

Traveling with your glider takes some real planning. It is much easier to rent a glider at the location and even cheaper to borrow one. Christy Huddle has written a good reference on this topic.

Will the airline even allow you to take your glider using a containerized cargo compartments? Of the U.S. airlines, only American Airlines will allow gliders or recognize them as oversized luggage. Before you buy your airline ticket, be sure to research the airline especially if you are going overseas. You will probably pay large fees going through customs or depending on the country large bribes.

Questions to ask the airlines before booking: Does the airline take hang gliders? Can your glider fit on the size of plane you will be using on all legs of the flight? Can your glider go through all airports you are travelling through? Is there an additional charge for the glider? How many pieces of luggage are you allowed to check through?
